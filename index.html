High school football: Landen Williams-Callis, David Gabriel-Georges headline MaxPreps National Player of the Year watch list

Find out who is in the running to join the list of past winners that includes Derrick Henry, Kyler Murray and Caleb Downs.

Thumbnail image of this article‚Äôs writer: Zack Poff

By Zack Poff

The 2025 high school football season is entering the postseason phase, meaning it is time to look at standout performers in the mix for MaxPreps National Player of the Year. Derrick Henry, Kyler Murray, Bryce Young, Jaxson Dart and Caleb Downs are some of the notable past winners of the award.

Tyler Atkinson of Grayson (Loganville, Ga.), Jared Curtis of Nashville Christian (Nashville, Tenn.) and Madden Williams of St. John Bosco (Bellflower, Calif.) are among the 10 players highlighted on the watch list. The race is still wide open, however, and players not featured on this list are not excluded from consideration for MaxPreps National Player of the Year, which will be named in January.

Atkinson has been a huge contributor for the No. 2 team in the MaxPreps Top 25 and after missing the first two games of the season. He leads the defending AAAAAA state champs in tackles (55), tackles for loss (18) and sacks (7) as they enter the playoffs 10-0.

Curtis was named the 2024 MaxPreps Tennessee Player of the Year and is putting together another remarkable season for the Eagles. He has thrown for 1,587 yards and 26 touchdowns to go with 44 carries for 392 yards and seven more scores while leading Nashville Christian to a 10-0 record.

Williams just broke St. John Bosco's school record hauling in his 27th career touchdown reception and has been a playmaker for the No. 4 team in the MaxPreps Top 25. He has had at least one receiving score in every game this season.

The list includes seven seniors, two juniors and one sophomore. Roye Oliver III of Hamilton (Chandler, Ariz.) was the lone player from the Class of 2028 selected, while David Gabriel-Georges of Baylor (Chattanooga, Tenn.) and Landen Williams-Callis of Randle (Richmond, Texas) were the two juniors.

Team success and individual production are the primary factors in determining MaxPreps National Player of the Year. The 10 candidates featured below play for programs with a 94-3 overall record and nine of them play for teams in our expanded top 100 rankings.
